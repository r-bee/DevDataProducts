Financial Statements and Statements of Operations

  • Financial Statements are reports that organizations are oblidged by the law to periodically publish.
  • There are several kinds of Financial Statements:
    • Balance Sheet: Displays the current situation of the organization, in terms of assets, liabilities and equity.
    • Income Statement or Statements of Operations: Display the revenues and expenses of the organization for a period of time.
    • Statement of Cash Flows: shows input and output for a period of time in terms of cash.
  • Here we will focus on the Statement of Operations for a hypothetical not-for-profit consutling company called VWX

Statement of Operations.

  • Contains several Reveneue and Expense categories.
  • Those interested in financially evaluating VWX want to see how Revenues and Expenses evolve over time.
    • For example if Revenues decrease over time, it means that the VWX might be heading to a financial trouble.
    • On the other hand, they may want to use the data to calculate various ratios, such as income-to-debt. For example if an organiztion has an yearly income $15,000,000 and a debt $50,000,000 the revenue-to-debt ratio would be:

Statement of Operations App

  • The VWX Statements of Operations App allows users display in a line-plot historical data for any set of expense or revenue category.
  • The user first selects the revenue and expense category she is interested in:
    • Revenues for VWX include: Service Contracts, Sales and (Other) Services, User Fees (for a web app they offer) or Investment Income.
    • Expeneses for VWX include: Salaries and Benefits (for paying staff), Operating Costs (IT, printing, travel) or taxes and utilities.

  • She then selects the time window for which she wants data to be displayed:

    • For VWX the available data are from 2004 to 2014
